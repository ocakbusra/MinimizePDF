How to Compress Government Report PDF for Archival

As a public official or clerk, transparency is your mandate. You need to upload last night's City Council meeting minutes and the new urban development report to the public website. But the content management system rejects the file because it's too large.

Government documents are historical records (Minutes, Agendas, Reports). They are often scanned at high resolution for archival purposes. However, making these accessible to the public on the web requires a different approach. Web servers have limits, and constituents shouldn't have to download a 100MB file to read a 10-page report.

Why File Size is a Problem for Officials

Scanning adds up. A 200-page agenda packet with scanned exhibits, maps, and letters can easily exceed 100MB. Most municipal websites and transparency portals have upload limits of 10-50MB. Furthermore, ADA compliance requires accessible documents, and huge scanned images are often not OCR'd or optimized for screen readers.
