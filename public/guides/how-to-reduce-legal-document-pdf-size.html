Why File Size is a Problem for Lawyers

Legal documents, especially those containing exhibits, act as "storage containers" for various types of data. You have typed briefs merged with scanned affidavits, high-resolution photos of evidence, and signed contracts. Scanners in law offices often default to 300 or 600 DPI, creating massive files that look good but are impractical digitally.

Systems like PACER, local court portals (e.g., UYAP), and case management software have strict upload limits to save server space. If your consolidated PDF is even 1MB over the limit, it will be rejected, forcing you to waste billable hours trying to split or re-scan documents.
