How to Optimize Design Portfolio PDF for Job Applications

You found the perfect job opening. You've tailored your resume. You attach your portfolio PDF, and... bounce. "Attachment too large." Most companies have a 10MB or 15MB limit on incoming emails.

Sending a Google Drive link is risky; overworked recruiters might not click it if they don't have permission or if it's blocked by IT. You want your work attached directly to the email so it's the first thing they see. You need a portfolio that is lightweight in bytes but heavy in visual impact.

Why File Size is a Problem for Designers

We know how it happens. You export from InDesign or Illustrator with "High Quality Print" settings because you want your work to look perfect. But digital viewing doesn't require 300 DPI ckyk images. A 50MB portfolio file is slow to download, slow to render, and annoying for a recruiter reviewing 100 applications a day.
