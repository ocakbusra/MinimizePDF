Why File Size is a Problem for Architects

Architectural PDFs are uniquely difficult to manage. They aren't just text; they are composed of thousands of complex vector paths, hatching patterns, and high-resolution renders layered on top of each other. A standard A1 or A0 sheet exported at high DPI for printing naturally results in a massive file size.

Clients and contractors often view these files on mobile devices or tablets on-site, where a 50MB file takes forever to load. Furthermore, most corporate email servers cap attachments at 20MB or 25MB. If your PDF exceeds this, it simply won't get through, leading to delays in the approval process and frustration for everyone involved.
