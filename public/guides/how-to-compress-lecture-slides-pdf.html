How to Compress PDF Lecture Slides for LMS Upload (Canvas/Moodle)

You want to upload your week's lecture slides to Canvas or BlackBoard so students can review them. But the file is rejected because it exceeds the course storage quota. You are left trying to split the presentation into 4 parts, confusing everyone.

Teaching materials are increasingly visual. You use high-quality images, diagrams, and scanned texts to engage students. However, Learning Management Systems (LMS) often have strict storage limits per course. Large files not only eat up this quota but are also difficult for students to download on slow home internet connections.

Why File Size is a Problem for Educators

Exporting PowerPoint or Keynote to PDF is standard practice to preserve formatting. However, these tools often default to print-quality outputs. A 50-slide deck can easily become 100MB. When you have 300 students trying to download that file simultaneously before an exam, it puts a strain on the network and frustrates learners.
