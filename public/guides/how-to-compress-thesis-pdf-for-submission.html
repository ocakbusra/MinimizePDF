Why File Size is a Problem for Researchers

Academic documents are unique. They aren't just text; they are dense compilations of vector graphs (from R, Python, or Matlab), high-resolution field photography, and scanned archival documents. When you export from LaTeX or Word, these assets are often embedded at full raw resolution.

University servers, however, are often outdated. Library systems set strict limits to save storage space. Reducing the file size without compromising the scientific integrity of your figures is a delicate balance.
